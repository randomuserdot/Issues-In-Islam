Bismillāh ar-Raḥmān ar-Raḥīm

This post will go into a deep research of understanding the šhirk and ʿibādah across several texts and interpretations of well known early scholars and later scholars who have adressed this issue for the student to know how one could fall into šhirk and avoid it, whilst understanding how other Muslims could be affected by a hidden form of šhirk that they do not recognise.


Allāh said:

وَمِنَ ٱلنَّاسِ مَن يَتَّخِذُ مِن دُونِ ٱللَّهِ أَندَادًۭا يُحِبُّونَهُمْ كَحُبِّ ٱللَّهِ ۖ وَٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوٓا۟ أَشَدُّ حُبًّۭا لِّلَّهِ ۗ وَلَوْ يَرَى ٱلَّذِينَ ظَلَمُوٓا۟ إِذْ يَرَوْنَ ٱلْعَذَابَ أَنَّ ٱلْقُوَّةَ لِلَّهِ جَمِيعًۭا وَأَنَّ ٱللَّهَ شَدِيدُ ٱلْعَذَابِ 


“Still there are some who take others as Allāh’s equal, they love them as they should love Allāh, but the ˹true˺ believers love Allāh even more. If only the wrongdoers could see the ˹horrible˺ punishment ˹awaiting them˺, they would certainly realize that all power belongs to Allah and that Allah is indeed severe in punishment.”


To understand what Allāh is trying to convey to us in this blessed verse, we have to understand what the interpretators said in order to grasp the nature of šhirk from this verse. However, we in first need to note what this verse establishes. This verse establishes a foundational principle in understanding the reality of šhirk (associating partners with Allāh). It identifies the essence of šhirk not merely in outward acts of worship to another being, object or so forth. Rather, inward orientation of the heart, specifically in directing a form of: reverence, love, etc. to others that is equal to how you would rever/love Allāh exclusively.

The verse describes a group of people who take rivals or equals besides Allāh, hence they love them as “as they love Allah.” The term 'nidd'  (نِدّ, its plural form being أنْداد) linguistically denotes something that is equal, comparable, or set in rivalry with another. Thus, the act of taking 'andād' entails elevating created beings or objects to a status that rivals Allāh in everything.

We derive this understanding from the great exegete (interpretor of text) Aṭ-Ṭabarī from his interpretation.

[He says:](https://shamela.ws/book/7798/1667)

“He, exalted is His praise, means by that: among people are those who take, besides Allah, rivals to Allah. We have already explained previously that a nidd (rival/equal) is something equal or comparable, with evidence indicating that, so we disliked repeating it. And those who have taken these rivals besides Allah love their rivals as the believers love Allah. Then He informed that the believers are stronger in their love for Allah than those who take these rivals are in their love for their rivals.”


This understanding from the greate exegete is shown from other figures of the early predecessors (as-Salaf) who have interpretated these verses for people to know and understand depthly. 

[Aṭ-Ṭabarī narrated as will come in the next page:](https://shamela.ws/book/7798/1667)

Bišhr ibn Muʿāḏh narrated: Yazīd narrated to us, from Saʿīd, from Qatada ibn Di'ama concerning the statement of Allāh: 

““And among the people are those who take, besides Allāh, rivals (andād), loving them as they love Allāh; but those who believe are stronger in love for Allāh” 

that is, from the disbelieverstoward their idols.”


Muḥammad ibn ʿAmr narrated: Abū ʿĀṣim narrated to us, from ʿĪsā, from Ibn Abī Najīḥ, from Mujahid ibn Jabr regarding the statement: 

““They love them as they love Allāh” He explained: this is out of rivalry and imitation, likening falsehood to the truth through these rivals. “But those who believe are stronger in love for Allāh” Meaning: the believers’ love for Allāh is stronger than the disbelievers’ love for their deities.”



Yūnus narrated: Ibn Wahb reported from Ibn Zayd concerning the same verse:

““And among the people are those who take besides Allāh rivals, loving them as they love Allāh”
he said: These are the polytheists. Their rivals (andād) are their deities which they worship alongside Allāh. They love them just as the believers love Allāh.
But those who believe are stronger in love for Allāh: than these [polytheists] (who) are in their love for their deities.”


[Ibn Taymiyyah said:](https://shamela.ws/book/22653/352)

“And this is because the reality of tawḥīd is that you do not love except Allāh, and that you love what Allāh loves, for the sake of Allāh. Thus, you do not love except for Allāh, nor hate except for Allāh.”


Ibn Taymiyyah also goes ahead to mention what worship essentially is, as we will also explain what it is later.



[He also said:](https://shamela.ws/book/21506/1997)

“Worship combines the perfection of love with the perfection of humility. So no one is a believer until Allāh is more beloved to him than everything besides Him, and that he worships Allāh sincerely making the religion purely for Him. This that he mentioned regarding the stations of the knowers is the first step that the believer places in īmān. One is not a believer if he does not possess this. The Salaf of the Ummah, its imāms, and its scholars have agreed that Allāh is loved for His very essence. None disputed this except a group from the people of kalām and opinion, who followed the path of the Jahmiyyah in some of their affairs, and said: “He neither loves nor is He loved.””


[as-Saʿdī said:](https://shamela.ws/book/42/114)


“With this complete clarification, whoever takes from the created beings rivals (andād) to Allāh, meaning equals and counterparts, equating them with Allāh in worship, love, glorification, and obedience: Whoever is in this state, after the proof has been established and tawḥīd clarified, it is known that he is opposing Allāh, in hostility against Him, or turning away from reflecting upon His signs and pondering His creation. So he has no excuse whatsoever. Rather, the word of punishment has become binding upon him. These who take rivals alongside Allāh do not equate them with Him in creation, provision, or governance, but rather they equate them with Him in worship so they worship them in order that they may bring them closer to Him.”


Altogether, from the understanding and statements of these scholars, we have come to understood a fundamental principle in understanding the nature of šhirk: it originates in ascribing to something a form of love that renders it comparable to Allāh. The redirection of such love to an object or deity constitutes the inner foundation of šhirk as we know it, thus it outwardly manifests in acts of worship directed to other than Allāh. 

This is why it is crucial to note that Ibn Taymiyyah mentioned that worship is both: humility and love towards only to Allāh in such manner, an ultimate love coupled with reverence and humility. This highlights how worship is also a comprehensive state of heart characterised by devotion, humility and exaltation. We then have deeper insight that this love is amongst the greatest obligations of faith, otherwise a person would not be a true believer, as this is extremely essential. 

Hence, love serves as the origin of every act within the religion, just as affirmation serves as the origin of every statement of faith.


[Ibn al-Qayyim said:](https://shamela.ws/book/6832/180)


“And love, together with submission, is the servitude for which the creation was created. For indeed it is the utmost degree of love combined with the utmost degree of humility.

And this is not befitting except for Him. And associating others with Him in this is the šhirk which Allāh does not forgive, nor does He accept from its doer any deed.”


In conclusion, worship is indeed both: humility and love towards Allāh only, in which He is to be ultimately revered and loved, unlike how the pagans fell into the inner foundations of šhirk.
